Human Vs. Animal Hair

Another type of hair sample that is useful in a criminal matter is animal hair.  Many people have domesticated animals as pets or for agricultural purposes, therefore if a unique animal hair is found at a crime scene and can be tied in with a suspect, it can prove to be a useful piece of corroborative evidence.

Human hairs tend to be consistent in color throughout the length of the hair shaft, but the color in animal hairs is found in bands randomly throughout the hair.  Also, human hairs tend to be slightly darker near the exterior of each hair (cuticle) while animal hairs tend to be darker near the  middle (medulla).
