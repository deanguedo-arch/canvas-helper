How are blood types identified once they're in the lab?

Scientists create serums containing Anti-A or Anti-B antibodies.  These serums are combined with the blood samples from the crime scene.

Blood type A will agglutinate when brought into contact with anti-A serum and blood type B will agglutinate when brought into contact with anti-B serum.  Blood type AB will agglutinate when in contact with both serums and blood type O will not agglutinate with either serum.

Using this simple test, we can be determine the blood type of the sample.
