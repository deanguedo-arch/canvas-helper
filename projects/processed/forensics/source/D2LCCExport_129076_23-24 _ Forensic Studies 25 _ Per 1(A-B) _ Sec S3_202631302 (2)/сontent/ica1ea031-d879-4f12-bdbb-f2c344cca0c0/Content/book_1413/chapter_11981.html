Normally, one investigator is in charge of supervising the collection of evidence to ensure it is completed efficiently and thoroughly.

Although rarely seen on TV, investigators often wear "protective suits" to collect evidence. These suits prevent anything on their bodies, such as hair, fibres, seat, and saliva, from combining with and/or contaminating the evidence collected. 

The evidence is collected gently not to destroy or contaminate it. Each piece of trace evidence is properly labeled with date, time, case number, and a brief description. The investigator double packages any trace evidence collected - a small container is placed inside a larger container. Both containers are then labeled. Double packaging ensures that, if the small container opens by accident, the larger outer container can prevent loss of evidence. 

Specialized vacuums like the one pictured are equipped with special filters and are used to collect trace evidence from crime scenes. Other tools used by investigators include forceps, cotton swabs, probes, tape, and various types of evidence containers such as plastic bags, envelopes, test tubes, paper bags, and re-sealable plastic containers.
