⚙️ Engine Tuning: Arousal & Anxiety

Theoretical foundations and the mechanics of performance activation.

⚡ Core Definitions

Arousal A neutral blend of physiological and psychological activity. It is the "intensity" of motivation on a continuum from deep sleep to intense excitement.
Anxiety A negative emotional state characterized by nervousness and worry. It includes Cognitive (mental worry) and Somatic (physical activation) components.
Trait vs. State

Trait is your stable personality; State is the "right now" feeling.

Facilitative

Interpreting pressure as positive and helpful for the task.

🔄 The 4-Stage Stress Process (McGrath)

1

Environmental Demand: A physical or psychological task placed on you.

2

Perception: How you interpret the demand (Threat vs. Challenge).

3

Stress Response: Increased state anxiety, muscle tension, and focus changes.

4

Consequences: Actual behavior/performance outcome.

🛠️ Phase 2: The Interventions Toolkit

Managing the engine: Centering, S.M.A.R.T. goals, and cognitive overrides.

🌬️ Centering & PMR

Centering is a diaphragmatic breathing manual override for the autonomic nervous system. It resets the stress loop and anchors the operator in the present.

Progressive Muscle Relaxation (PMR): Systematically tensing and releasing muscle groups to recognize the difference between tension and relaxation.

🧠 Cognitive Restructuring

Thought Stopping: Using a physical or verbal trigger to clear negative self-talk, followed by reframing.

Negative State

"I can't believe I missed that. I'm going to blow the lead."

Instructional Reframe

"Stop. Next play. Keep my eyes on the rim. Finish."

🎯 S.M.A.R.T. Goal Focus

Outcome Goals

Focus on results (winning, beating others). While motivating, these often increase anxiety because the performer lacks 100% control over the outcome.

Process Goals

Focus on specific actions (elbow tucked, breathing pattern). These reduce anxiety because the performer has full control over execution.

👤 Phase 3: The Operator

Identity Status, Motivation Profiles, and Fatigue Spectrum.

🛡️ Identity Status (Marcia)

Identity Foreclosure

Prematurely committing to an identity (identifying only as an athlete) without exploration. High risk for crisis during injury or retirement.

Intrinsic

Satisfaction from the game itself. Stable long-term.

Extrinsic

Driven by status/praise. High risk of burnout.

🛌 The Fatigue Spectrum

Rest is not just absence of activity; it is multidimensional (Social, Intellectual, Psychological).

Overtraining Short cycle near max load
Under-recovery Resource/Demand imbalance
Burn-out Psychophysiological withdrawal
