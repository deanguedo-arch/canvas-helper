Instructions for Submitting Assignment Booklets

1. Submit Assignment Booklets regularly for correction.
2. Submit only one Assignment Booklet at one time. This allows your teacher to
provide helpful comments that you can apply to subsequent course work and
exams (if applicable).
3. Check the following before submitting each Assignment Booklet:
a. Are all assignments complete?
b. Have you edited your work to ensure accuracy of information and details?
c. Have you proofread your work to ensure correct grammar, spelling, and
punctuation?
d. Did you complete the Assignment Booklet cover and attach the correct label?

Postal Mail

(Envelopes less than two centimetres thick receive the most economical rate.)

Alternate Methods

 2. Using the sleep survey that follows, question/survey at least four individuals over the
age of 12 (e.g., parents, siblings, other relatives, friends, neighbors, etc.), then respond
to the items that follow.  Make sure to record their responses in the following table.

Sleep Survey
Q1.
Snoring occurs when there is an obstruction to the free flow of air through the passages at
the back of the mouth and nose.

A: TRUE – This area is the collapsible part of the airway where the tongue and upper
throat meet the soft palate and uvula.  Snoring occurs when these structures strike
each other and vibrate during breathing.
Q2.
Ten percent of adults snore regularly.

A: FALSE – Current studies indicate that approximately 25% of adults sore regularly.
Q3.
Snoring is often an indication of a medical problem.

A: TRUE – When snoring is severe, it can be associated with serious, long-term health
problems, including obstructive sleep apnea.
Q4.
Obstructive sleep apnea occurs when breathing stops completely due to an obstructed
airway.
 A: TRUE – When loud snoring is interrupted by frequent episodes of totally
obstructed breathing, it is known as obstructive sleep apnea.
Q5.
Having an alcoholic beverage before bed can help people relax so they can have a good
night’s sleep.

A: FALSE – Alcohol may help a person enter light sleep, but it prevents a person from
entering REM sleep
Q6.
Beverages containing caffeine, such as coffee, can cause insomnia.

A: TRUE – Caffeine is a stimulant.
Q7.
Teens need just as much sleep as babies do.

A: FALSE – On average, babies need 16 hours of sleep per day while teens need 9
hours.
Q8.
When tested on a driving simulator, people who are sleep deprived perform just as poorly
as individuals who are intoxicated.
 A: TRUE – And the worst combination is someone who is both sleep deprived and
intoxicated!
Q9.
Narcolepsy is a sudden sleep attack in the middle of being awake.

A: TRUE – Individuals cannot control the onset of a sleep attack.
Q10.
Narcolepsy is caused by not getting enough sleep.
 A: FALSE – A person with narcolepsy can still experience sleep attacks despite
receiving sufficient sleep the night before.
