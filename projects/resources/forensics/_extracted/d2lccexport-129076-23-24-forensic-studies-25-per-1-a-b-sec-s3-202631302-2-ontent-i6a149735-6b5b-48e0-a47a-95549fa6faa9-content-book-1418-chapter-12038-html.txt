Writing StyleAnother way to individualize a piece of writing is to examine its overall style, the way the writing on the page is arranged. The way a person organizes writing on a page is a personal habit and a distinctive part of personal writing style. These samples are then compared to the original criminal document. Sound easy? Unfortunately, obtaining a useful and valid writing sample from a suspect is actually much more difficult than it sounds because a suspect can easily consciously alter his or her writing.Some steps taken by forensic experts to obtain useful comparative writing samples and to minimize the alteration of writing samples includehaving the suspect write several pagesusing the same or similar type of paper as the suspect documentusing the same or similar writing instrument as used in the suspect documenthaving the suspect use many of the words and sentences found in the suspect documentnot providing any assistance with spelling, punctuation, or with use of lower or upper case letterstelling the suspect what to write (verbal dictation)having the suspect write the same verbal dictation three separate times
