How are blood types identified once they're in the lab?Scientists create serums containing Anti-A or Anti-B antibodies.  These serums are combined with the blood samples from the crime scene.Blood type A will agglutinate when brought into contact with anti-A serum and blood type B will agglutinate when brought into contact with anti-B serum.  Blood type AB will agglutinate when in contact with both serums and blood type O will not agglutinate with either serum.Using this simple test, we can be determine the blood type of the sample.The following image shows the distribution of blood types in North America.   So unfortunately having a blood type is not enough to pin down a suspect.  Before DNA testing, there were 5 or 6 different blood typing systems that were used to further identify the blood.
