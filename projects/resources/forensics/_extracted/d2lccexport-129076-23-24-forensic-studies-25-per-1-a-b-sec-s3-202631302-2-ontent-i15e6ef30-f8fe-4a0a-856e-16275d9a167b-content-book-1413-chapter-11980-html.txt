When forensic experts examine a piece of trace evidence with a microscope, they first identify it. To do this, they determine if the evidence is from an organic (natural) source or inorganic (synthesis and/or man-made) source. After a piece of trace evidence has been identified, investigators attempt to indiviualize it. this usually involves linking the evidence with known suspects. Sometimes this is successful, but often the eidence cannot be linked directly to a suspect. Successfully individualizing a piece of evidence results from a combination of luck and skill of investigators. If a piece of evidence is identified but cannot be individualized, it still is used in the case. However, more evidence is needed to charge and convict a suspect.
